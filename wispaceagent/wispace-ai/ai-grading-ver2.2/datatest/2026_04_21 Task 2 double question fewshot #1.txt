As major cities in the world are growing today, so do their problems. What are the problems for young people who are living in the cities as a result of continued growth? What are the solutions for these problems?

In today's world, urbanization and globalization have become inevitable trends that profoundly affect the development of major cities. This could be associated with multiple issues, with a heavy impact placed on the urban youth, which will be analyzed in the essay below, together with several solutions that could be taken into consideration. 
There are two problems emerging as urban centers grow globally. The most striking one is the deterioration in health conditions among young urban dwellers on the grounds that the more the metropolises expand, the more trees are cut down to make room for infrastructural growth. There is a strong likelihood that not only would airborne pollutant levels increase, but young citizens may also be exposed to these pollutants frequently, rendering them more vulnerable to respiratory diseases such as sinusitis, asthma, and lung cancer, in the worst-case scenario. In addition to this is that young urbanites may face financial pressure due to the requirement of adapting to the high living cost in urban areas. More specifically, they have to cover their basic necessities and housing expenses, which are two major financial burdens to young adults.
To combat these, two methods as follow can be put into practice. To begin with, it is necessary for governments to implement policies to reduce the influx of the population into the city by virtue of the fewer people migrating to urban centers, the less public purse authorities need to allocate for the upgrade of infrastructure, allowing more resources for social welfare programs instead. For instance, government subsidies can be distributed to healthcare and housing, giving rise to the alleviation of economic adversity for the young individuals. This is very likely to help them ensure more financially comfortable lives, especially those who have not reached stability yet.
In conclusion, the potential repercussions of the fact that urbanization and globalization are manifold. Fortunately, they could be addressed by a number of resolutions that require cooperation from many stakeholders.
