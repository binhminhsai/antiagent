The best way to solve the world’s environmental problem is to increase the price of fuel. To what extent do you agree or disagree?

It has become increasingly prevalent to observe a number of environmental problems happening in the world, so some believe that elevating the price of gasoline is the best way to address those problems. From my standpoint, however, I will challenge this school of thought for several reasons, which will be presented in the following essay.t
On the one hand, there is a significant reason why increasing the fuel cost may be advantageous. The most striking one is the potential for the fuel emission to be reduced. To be more specific, less released fuel consumption resulting from affordability issues not only can render the environment fresher, but can also force many people to opt for public transport. This, according to the theory, ultimately results in less air pollution being recorded. However, this line of reasoning is not sound on the grounds that the percentage of pollution caused by fuel emission is negligible, compared to ones caused by public awareness such as littering, cutting trees,... This fact justifies that the effect of increasing the price of fuel is unremarkable.
On the other hand, I believe that there are more effective solutions to tackle environmental problems. To add further credence to my assertion, I note the root cause of most ecological issues: human activities. This could be exemplified in many developing countries, where people’s literacy is low, which is very likely to littering as well as illegal dumping or trash burning. Hence, the key to solving environmental problems is to raise public awareness, especially about how themselves and the world would be adversely affected by those issues. This could be done by launching advocacy community consciousness campaigns through schools, offices and villages, thereby equipping preserving skills for residents and decreasing the problems caused by daily activities. Coupled with this is the fact that if awareness is not raised, there is a high probability that elevating fuel cost would be neglected, and people may still continue buying fuel due to high demand of travelling.
